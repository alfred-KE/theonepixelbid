Manifesto

The Internet is infinite — billions of pages, trillions of pixels; an ocean of color and code.

But somewhere in this infinity, there must be a beginning. A single, finite point from which everything could start — and end.

That point is here. A single pixel. The smallest possible unit of digital existence. The atom of our online universe.

For thirty years, pixels have built our world — our memories, our identities, our economies. They have shaped desires, sold dreams, captured faces. And yet, no one has ever owned one. Not symbolically. Not truly. Until now.

The Auction. This is the first and last pixel ever to be auctioned. Visible to all, owned by one. A paradox of digital rarity — public and private, infinite and unique.

The winning bidder will not just own a pixel, but a symbol — of value, attention, and the absurd beauty of the Internet itself.
